<p><p>Electron Beam Lithography (EBL) is a high-resolution technique used to create patterns on a substrate for applications in semiconductors and nanotechnology. The market for EBL is categorized into two primary types based on electron source technology: thermionic sources and field electron emission sources. Thermionic sources use heated filaments to generate electrons, while field emission sources utilize strong electric fields for electron extraction, allowing for improved resolution and throughput. Both types cater to differing needs in precision, speed, and cost in various applications.</p></p>
