<p><p>The Cassava Modified Starch market features several key types based on the modification processes applied. Acetation involves the addition of acetyl groups to enhance thermal stability and clarity. Phosphate modification improves emulsification and texture. Hydroxypropylation increases water solubility and resistance to retrogradation. Oxidation alters starch's granular structure for better functional properties. Cross-linking enhances resistance to heat and shear, making it suitable for various applications. The "Others" category includes novel modifications that broaden the starch's usability in food and industrial sectors.</p></p>
