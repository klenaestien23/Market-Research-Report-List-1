<p><ul><li>Spectral Domain OCT</li><li>Swept Source OCT</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Optical Coherence Tomography (OCT) in ophthalmology includes two main types: Spectral Domain OCT and Swept Source OCT. Spectral Domain OCT utilizes light frequency analysis to produce high-resolution cross-sectional images of the retina, making it effective for diagnosing various eye conditions. Swept Source OCT employs a tunable laser to provide deeper tissue penetration, allowing for rapid imaging and enhanced visualization of the posterior segment. Both technologies are essential for early detection and management of ocular diseases.</p></p>
