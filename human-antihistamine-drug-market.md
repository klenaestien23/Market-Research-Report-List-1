<p><p>The human antihistamine drug market is primarily categorized into H-1 and H-2 receptor antagonists. H-1 receptor antagonists are commonly used to treat allergic reactions, hay fever, and motion sickness by blocking the effects of histamine on blood vessels and nerves. In contrast, H-2 receptor antagonists are mainly utilized to reduce gastric acid production, addressing conditions like peptic ulcers and gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD). Both categories play crucial roles in managing various allergic and gastrointestinal disorders.</p></p>
