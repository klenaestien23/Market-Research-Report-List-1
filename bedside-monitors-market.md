<p><p>The bedside monitors market is categorized into three main types based on patient acuity levels. High-acuity monitors are designed for critically ill patients, providing advanced monitoring of vital signs and multiple parameters. Mid-acuity monitors are suitable for general medical and surgical patients, offering essential monitoring capabilities without the complexity of high-acuity devices. Low-acuity monitors are used for stable patients, typically in outpatient or post-operative settings, focusing on basic vital signs monitoring and ensuring patient safety in less intensive environments.</p></p>
