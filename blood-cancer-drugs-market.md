<p><p>The blood cancer drugs market encompasses two primary categories: chemotherapy drugs and targeted therapy drugs. Blood chemotherapy drugs are designed to kill rapidly dividing cancer cells, often affecting healthy cells, and are typically administered intravenously or orally. In contrast, blood targeted therapy drugs focus on specific molecular targets associated with cancer cells, minimizing damage to healthy tissues and enhancing treatment efficacy. This precision approach often leads to improved outcomes and reduced side effects for patients suffering from various types of blood cancers.</p></p>
