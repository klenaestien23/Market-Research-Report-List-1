<p><p>The seaweed gum market consists of three primary types: alginates, agar, and carrageenan. Alginates, derived from brown algae, are commonly used as a thickening and gelling agent in food and pharmaceutical applications. Agar, extracted from red algae, serves as a gelling agent, especially in microbiological culture media and desserts. Carrageenan, another red algae derivative, is utilized mainly for its thickening and stabilizing properties in dairy products and processed foods. Together, they play crucial roles across various industries.</p></p>
